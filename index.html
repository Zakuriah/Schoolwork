    Project Plan

    Overview

    My project is an app which shows different configurations and options of computers which will be for gaming. The app will also present step-by-step instructions on how to build a PC containing videos and graphics to help first-time builders. The configurations will show the cost, part name and location of where the parts can be bought. As well as daily updates on deals for those particular parts from Australian stores. Its parts can also be changed to cater to the users if they want to upgrade or downgrade its performance. The configurations will also present different performance diagrams and tables which helps users compare other configurations and their different advantages.

    Motivation

    People who are seeking to build and buy their first computer do a lot of research of many different parts, configurations and prices because of its their first computer and want it to last for many years until the next iteration of new generations of hardware. They will also go to youtube and watch computer building creators like LinusTechTips, Jay2Cents, etc so they can build their first computer from the parts they’ve bought. They’ve spent so much time on research that it can span over days or weeks in planning before they feel that they’re comfortable to do it, or they may not even be comfortable at all. Having an app like this cuts time and encourages users to get hands-on with their computers.

    Description

    The project will consist of 3 major parts: computer configurations and builds, how-to videos and community forums. The page will consist of 3 different configurations for the user to choose from:

    • Gaming
    • Build-your-own
    • How-to
    • Community
    The 'Gaming' will have 3 standard configurations for the users to view: budget, mid-tier, and high-tier. Budget will cater to users who will play e-sports titles (CS-GO, Dota 2, LOL, Rocket League, etc) and some old AAA games. The cost of the budget configuration will be $750. Mid-tier is for users who will comfortably play AAA games at medium-high settings and easily run e-sports titles. The cost of the mid-tier configuration will be $1500. High-tier is for users who want to play their games at ultra setting and more such as rendering. The cost of the high-tier will be $2500. All of the configurations will be updated constantly due to the different parts being cheaper.

    Build-your-own allows users to build their own system on the app. It has categories for parts (example hard drives & SSDs) and are subcategories by what type of part they want (example 2.5’ drives or 3.5’ drives). All of the parts will have all of the costs listed from different Australian outlets supplying the parts. On completion, the PC configuration will display different averages of FPS of e-sports titles and AAA games and can compare different builds if the user decides on a part they want to change.

    How-to provides topics of how to build a computer and troubleshoot a computer, which contains short and sharp instructions, graphics and a video for each topic. The ‘build-a-computer’ contains subcategories of different parts and users choose which parts they have as users may or may not have a particular part. It is difficult to have one video that can cater to any build as every system is different. By doing this, it makes it much easier and efficient way to find what you need. The ‘troubleshooting-a-computer’ shows users how to troubleshoot a computer which includes: overclocking, installing OEMs, raid, network connection set-up, etc. There is also a list in the topics that contain further information such as why that error occurs and how it occurs.

    Description

    Community allows users to discuss different topics and threads, they could be:

    • Community builds
    • Help forum
    • Community discussion
    • FAQ
    • Bugs & glitches
    • Patch news
    The reason why to have a community forum is it allows users to connect and speak to other users that may have a question or problem they need an answer to. It also allows users to voice out any bugs, glitches or ideas for the app which could potentially improve it. The forums are checked by admins to regulate the posts that may go against the terms and service which can include inappropriate use, bullying etc.

    Tools and Technologies

    For the app to be created, I need an app creation software such as Adobe XD and Android Studio that allows me to create the app and build the interface with the help of UX consultants. Of course money because the app needs to be R&D prototyped, tested, experimented and advertised.

    Skills Required

    The list of skills to build the app:

    • Proficiency in app development software (android and IOS)
    • UX development
    • Computer proficiency
    • Knowledge and experience of experimentation and feedback
    • Phones to test the app
      • These are just scratching the surface of the skills needed to build the app.

    Outcome

    The outcome would be that the user will now have a hub on their phone which helps users with computers and how to build them. It will also cut their research time greatly hopefully as there are already different configurations and community configurations that’ll give them information and help with their part choice. The app’s goal is to educate and encourage users of technology, specifically computers now that we are at an era where technology is everywhere and it can be quite a handful for some users. Hopefully that would be the impact of the app.
